About Cyberdyne Systems

Cyberdyne systems started as a small manufacturing company during the tech boom of the early 1980's, with it's first plant opening in 1984.

Despite it's humble beginnings, Cyberdyne would combine the keen strategic foresight of future orientated thinkers with an aggressively relentless commitment to technological innovation, becoming within a few short years the premier global supplier of military technology to the western world.

Military Applications

Skynet was created by Cyberdyne Systems for SAC-NORAD (Strategic Air Command-North American Aerospace Defense Command). After reverse engineering the CPU of a piece of recovered proprietary technology and created a powerful new microprocessor for weapons systems, becoming a major contractor for the US military. Skynet was developed as a network of supercomputers that employs artificial intelligence in order to replace human beings as commercial and military aircraft pilots, and for the control of other military systems. The application of technological solutions to military demands allows the redirection of human resources to innovation and reduces the risk of violence and trauma incurred by human soldiers in traditional warfare.
